About Me

In pursuit of that goal, in 2017 I published my first mobile app (Motive) that helps friends accomplish goals together. In my spare time I volunteer and serve as a board member for the AAMAA and Himmati Foundation nonprofit groups.

Natcar Autonomous Vehicle

In my senior year my team and I designed a line-following autonomous vehicle. We used an FRDM-KL25Z board as the microcontroller, printed the PCB that housed the half bridges for our motors, and built the camera mechanically from the ground up. The main sensors we used were a 128 pixel linescan camera and velocity wheel encoders. We were able to reach an average track speed of nearly 8.4 ft/second. For the steering algorithm we used PD control. Check out a quick powerpoint presentation on our project.

Autonomous Trash Can

This project designs and implements an autonmous moving trash can that will calculate the trajectory of a thrown trash projectile, actuate the motors of a moving base, and attempt to catch the trash projectile. The sensor(s) utilized will be an Xbox Kinect Camera. Image and depth data from the Kinect Camera will be sent to a MacOS computer to determine the trajectory of the trash projectile in 3D space. Location of the trash can will be implemented via AR (Augmented Reality) tags using ROS (Robot Operating System). The MacOS computer will then determine the appropriate motor speed and wheel direction to reach the projected destination of the trash projectile and transmit this information to an Arduino on the moving trash can via Bluetooth Low Energy. Read more technical details in our report.

Difference of Gaussian Filter

For our final project, my partner and I have implemented a difference-of-Gaussians image filter using a Xilinx FPGA platform. Difference-of-Gaussians is an algorithm that enhances the features of a given data input. In the context of our project, which receives an image as input, the difference-of-Gaussians filter highlights the image’s key points—that is, the locations which best define the image. Selecting these points is necessary for many types of image processing. The motivation for our Gaussian filtration is the SIFT (Scale-Invariant Feature Tracking) system, in which key points are defined and maintained given a moving image. Though the SIFT implementation itself proved too difficult to include in the final checkpoint, we still learned a great deal about its concepts and mechanics.Read our final report here.

Electroencephalography

Used Multisim to design an EEG from the RLC level with an instrumentation amplifier, low pass filter, and a 60 Hz notch filter to filter out noise. Then used Ultiboard to create a PCB design for the Multisim design, and had it printed. Soldered each resistor, capacitor, and op amp individually and tested aggressively using oscilloscopes. Board was accurately able to read professor's brain activity.

Excelerate Test Prep

Excelerate provides comprehensive data analysis solutions to standardized test prep centers. Excelerate takes a students' test results and delivers them to the cloud while providing in depth analysis into a student's vulnerabilities and points of improvement using basic linear regression and clustering. Excelerate won the 2014 Chapman University Business Plan Competition.

TensorFlow NFL Quarterback Projection

Made major improvements and modifications to the chained simulator including parallel injection, payload bit storage for flits, latency verification, tx and rx rate enforcement, and network layer arbitration. Simulator was built to correspond with real RTL modules. Packaged these modules into a cycle approximate standalone C++ functional model. Patent for simulator is pending.
